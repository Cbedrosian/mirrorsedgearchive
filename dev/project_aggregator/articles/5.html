Mirror’s Edge Catalyst – A Love Letter

Most of the time I try to avoid writing about specific titles on this blog. Mostly because I didn’t intend it to be a review blog. I don’t have the strict, unbiased approach a good reviewer should have. I can’t just turn off my hype and my emotions, and objectively list pros and cons of a title. At least I can’t do that without writing the most boring, lifeless text of all time.

On the other hand I created this blog with the intention of writing about things that are on my mind. Without having any specific rules about the topics, and form. And at this point I realized my mind keeps going back to Mirror’s Edge Catalyst like a little anchor in my happy place. And I will not rest easy until I share how I feel about this game. It’s not going to be an actual review, I’m not gonna explain the story, list qualities. I just want to explain how it makes me feel.

Just to get it out of the way. I am aware that EA is considered an evil company full of evil people with evil ideas. And I’m not gonna deny there’s sins they should atone for. But I’m not gonna judge a game based on how evil or good the company is. And I’m not gonna pretend there aren’t any technical issues and glitches in the game either. I have encountered some, maybe two crashes and a few deadly glitches, that threw my character off a building and into death. Not too many, not to a point, where it was frustrating and made it impossible to enjoy the game though.

And lastly, before I get to the feeling part of the post, I do want to address one thing. Mirror’s Edge Catalyst was created as a reboot of the franchise, not an actual prequel to the existing Mirror’s Edge title. And that actually does bother me a lot. Because rebooting is just another form of retcon, which I’ve written about before. A lot. In my opinion, retcon is evil. In my opinion, reboots are just retcon in disguise. And therefore also evil, and sneaky. And if I was gonna change anything about the game, I would make it an actual prequel to Mirror’s Edge, instead of a reboot.

That being said, there are a few games in this world that make me feel the way Mirror’s Edge Catalyst makes me feel.

In general I gravitate towards games that are story based, and provide a number of specific missions to do. I am not a fan of open world. The more open the world of the game, the less interest I’m probably going to have. I get bored with open worlds, I don’t know how to keep myself occupied in them. Skyrim is death to me. After searching through my tenth ruin or cave I just lose interest. And the story is just.. I guess not strong enough.

So why do I keep coming back to Mirror’s Edge Catalyst, without any specific quest to do. Why do I turn the game on for 15 minutes, and just aimlessly run around the City of Glass, loving every minute of it?

In short… this game is like something stolen from my dreams, and conjured into gaming reality. Everything about this game is a dream come true for me. It’s not just something I want, it’s something my brain needs to feel better.

Starting with the gameplay and the entire idea of the game being basically an awesome free running simulation. I’ve always felt free-running is an amazing sport, and dreamt of a body that could actually perform these amazing stunts. Not for the cool factor of doing awesome stunts. But for the ability to freely move through a city, over rooftops, and such, experiencing the smoothness and fluidity of movement.

Alas, I am not blessed with any coordination. I’m like a baby deer trying to walk most of the time. Clumsy doesn’t even begin to describe it. Even though I work out hard to gain a strong body, I will never be able to try free running for myself. But I still dream of the freedom and beauty of movement.

I’m sure many will disagree, but I don’t care what “many” have to say. To me, Mirror’s Edge Catalyst is the only acceptable substitute for actual free running. The movement, and controls in the game make the experience wonderfully fluid and seamless. It is difficult to describe the rush of endorphins I felt, when I unlocked most of Faith’s moves, and was able to just run through the city for more than 15 minutes, without stopping once. Jumping, rolling, climbing, without any pause, any hesitation, moving smoothly through the City of Glass. There were moments, when I expected to feel the wind on my face.

The game also implemented a wonderful feature that means if you run for a while without breaking the flow, the colors of the entire city appear more vivid. What can I say. If you see me playing this game, it’s guaranteed you will see me smiling at the screen. And that’s a rare sight.

But the motion itself would be nothing if it wasn’t for the absolutely stunning City of Glass. I don’t care what anybody says. I don’t even want to hear complaints. Cause I experience an eyegasm every single time I play it. Going through most of the game I was seduced by the gorgeous and yet simplistic design of the city.

In black, and white, silver, yellow, red and blue. Everything shiny, and beautiful and treacherous. Oh how easy it is to mistake a rooftop, with a huge gap between buildings, when everything is black and white. I love that thrill, but also the feeling of safety and pride when I finally got to know the city well enough to run through it without a doubt in my mind.

And after the game already seduced me with its design, I entered the Ocean Pier, and Regatta Bay areas for the first time

If previously it was eyegasming, then at this point playing Mirror’s Edge Catalyst was like climaxing during tantric sex.

Now the music in my opinion was way better in the original Mirror’s Edge. But the same person created the music for this game, and it’s still a lovely, if somewhat less impressive soundtrack. And the way the music grows a bit louder and more intense, when you keep the flow going for a while is superb. That, in addition with the colors getting brighter, and the endorphins hitting? Gaming heaven.

I have also completely fallen in love with the character design in the game. Even if some of the runners in the game made me laugh a little bit, with how stereotypically emo their style seemed to me. The “employs” are perfectly professional, clean, and simple. Almost hermetic. The runners are rebellious, dark, and full of energy. Including Noah, who seems to be the oldest (aside from Birdman) positive character around.

And Faith. Faith is absolutely gorgeous. Faith is my newest, and strongest girl crush. She is designed flawlessly. Beautiful, but not in a boring way. Graceful, a bit pouty, and… thank you Maker!.. not over sexualized. At all.

Playing Mirror’s Edge Catalyst calms me down. Makes me feel happy inside. Makes my anxiety shut up for a while, and gives me a little rush of endorphins when I’m feeling down. It’s enjoyable both through gameplay, and just through pleasing my senses of sight. I do not forbid anyone from complaining about the game if it has disappointed them in some way, but please do not frown upon me for loving it as much as I do. Finding something that has so far helped with my bad days every single time is rare for me. It’s a treasure.

I will keep coming back, as long as the game makes me feel this way. And I hope everyone finds something that makes them feel this way too.
