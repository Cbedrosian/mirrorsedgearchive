Mirror's Edge Catalyst: 'It's All New, It's All Different' Says Dev

By Bob Fekete

Mirror's Edge Catalyst is looking to kickstart the 2016 gaming year with a Feb. 23 release date. While fans such as myself are excited to play a new Mirror's Edge game, the story seemed a little confusing. After a recent demo in New York City, iDigitalTimes got the chance to talk with developers at DICE, who didn't really clear anything up.

When asked if Mirror's Edge Catalyst would serve as a reboot, sequel, re-imagining or something else, James Slavin responded saying it would serve as a "Rebirth." Slavin is the Audio Director on Mirror's Edge Catalyst.

"We share Faith's origin story," Slavin said. When pressed further, Slavin refused to give out any more details. "I will not talk about the story," he said. He did promise that what we would be playing is something he's excited for.

"It's all different, it's all new," Slavin said and mentioned the biggest difference between  Mirror's Edge Catalyst and the original game is the use of the Frostbite Engine. "[Frostbite] has allowed us to enhance the aspects that we want to enhance. Movement is more fluid, the city is bigger. Now we have room to tell this great story." All of this adds up to create a world that feels more alive than the first Mirror's Edge. "It lets you immerse yourselves in the world more," he said.

Another major difference is the elimination of weapons. In the first Mirror's Edge, players were rewarded for finishing the game without firing a gun. This time around, players don't even get the option of firing one.

"Faith is cool because she doesn't need weapons," Slavin explained. "She uses her body and the environment to her advantage. It's about you keeping your momentum, and running, and wall running, and jumping, and landing into someone. It's all about that mode of attack." This doesn't mean that Faith will be helpless. In fact, it's quite the opposite. "Faith is no super hero, but she's got guts and determination, so don't mess with her," Slavin said.

In other news, pre-orders have started for the Mirror's Edge Catalyst Collector's Edition. Alongside the confirmation people can go pre-order a copy for themselves, developer DICE has revealed the price and what comes along with a copy of Mirror's Edge Catalyst. Check it out below.

The Collector's Edition of Mirror's Edge Catalyst

Included in the Collector's Edition is the following:

  • Adult Faith/Young Faith diorama
  • Exclusive Steel book
  • Exclusive lithograph
  • Set of 10 concept pieces
  • Temporary logo tattoo
  • Temporary Faith arm tattoo
  • Storage box

The Faith diorama will be 14 inches tall, and shows what the main heroine of Mirror's Edge Catalyst looks like both as an adult and a young child. The temporary tattoos resemble the ones that Faith and her runner friends all share. 

The Mirror's Edge Catalyst Collector's Edition is priced at $199.99 and is available to pre-order at GameStop locations in North America. The Collector's Edition will be releasing on Xbox One, PS4, and PC. Other retailers worldwide will be announced soon, according to DICE.

In addition to getting the Collector's Edition, pre-ordering Mirror's Edge Catalyst will also earn you either the Combat Runner Kit or the Speed Runner Kit. If you instead pre-order Mirror's Edge Catalyst for PC through EA's Origin service, you will have access to both of the Combat Runner Kit and Speed Runner Kit.
