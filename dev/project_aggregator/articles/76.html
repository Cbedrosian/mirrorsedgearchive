Mirror’s Edge New Features Detailed


on 11 February, 2016 at 12:50 pm

As fans wait for the May 24th release date for the sequel to the 2008 original Mirror’s Edge, Sara Jansson, Senior Producer on Mirror’s Edge Catalyst spoke to The Examiner about things fans can expect to see in the sequel.

Due to the unique level design and traversal system she said it will change the play-style throughout the game.

“Since we’re building a city instead of a bunch of linear levels, I think the biggest thing is it’s more about creating a space that is going to be fun to traverse in a bunch of different directions,” Jansson explained of the level design, “That was never really the case in the first game, it was more about going from here to there.”




“Players also go from the free-roam into a building and while there is a lot of fluid movement when you’re on the rooftops,” said Jansson, “when you go into a building it becomes a little bit slower and more costly to figure out a way to get up or out of it.”

These locked off puzzle sections will also most likely serve as the points in which the story is pushed forwards.

But the truly great thing is, skill that was developed in the first game will transfer to the sequel, as all of Faith’s original moves are present from the start, but won’t be taught all at once. She stated you can “start using [Faith’s] moves immediately and they might be able to take other routes that new players can’t.”

“All of the basic movement you recognize from the first game is going to be available from the start, but that doesn’t mean that we teach from the start,” said Jansson, “New players might not even know moves are there, and we are not going to force you to use them early on.”

“We then try to make sure we spread that out a little bit more,” said Jansson “so we don’t push all of the new moves on the player in the first couple of minutes in the game.”

But while all the original moves are present from the start, that doesn’t mean that Faith won’t develop new skills and abilities that will allow her to unlock new areas of the city.




“There will be upgrades for you but they will be more related to your glove,” Jansson detailed, “That is more about expanding your tools so you can reach new areas of the city. That’s how we open up more areas of the city to the player.”

Then we have the combat, which is a greater extension of the movement, so you’re going to be able to learn more new moves and attacks over time

“Then we have the combat, which is a greater extension of the movement, so you’re going to be able to learn more new moves and attacks over time,” said Jansson, “At its core the combat is about how you combine the use of the environment with your momentum, your moves and with attacks, but gamers are also going to be able to expand those attacks that you have.”

Balancing a first person melee combat system can be hard because there are so few examples in Triple A games. The AI has been reworked over and over to become more tactical.

“It’s completely new AI and the major difference is it has had to work with the new type of combat,” said Jansson, “In this game it’s about more tactical AI.”




She outlined three different enemy types as well, the grunts to start off with, Shooters, and Heavies, which will be introduced throughout the progression of the game.

Outside of the main story and character progression there is of course side missions. An open world game wouldn’t be open without side quests.

“The side content is focused on the different pillars of the game,” Jansson said of the distractions players can find, “It’s not about mini games, but more so about building on combat, movement and hustling in the environment.”

It’s ramping up to be an exciting game!
