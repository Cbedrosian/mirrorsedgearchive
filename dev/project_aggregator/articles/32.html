Solar Fields – Mirror’s Edge Catalyst

In spite of unfavorable reviews, Mirror’s Edge Catalyst has garnered much acclaim among gamers thanks to its soundtrack produced by Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson). The composer, who specializes in electronica, has not failed his fans and once again built the atmosphere of dynamic movement which accompany the “runner” named Faith. In our interview the artist has decided to share his thoughts on the soundtracks for both Mirror’s Edge games and revealed when he is coming to Poland.

„Have no specific style, when I get inspired I hear music/sounds/melodies in my head, or sometimes it is a feeling I like to converting to music.” – Solar Fields

gamemusic.pl: How would you describe your style of writing music? Would you attribute that style to one of the classical music composers?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Have no specific style, when I get inspired I hear music/sounds/melodies in my head, or sometimes it is a feeling I like to converting to music. One small sound harmonic leads to something else, this is how I compose.

gamemusic.pl: In Mirror’s Edge Catalyst we hear a lot more classic instruments in comparison to the previous installment of the game. Could you tell us a little more about the selection of instruments and what impact it had on the soundtrack creation process?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): I wouldn’t say a lot of more classical instruments, it is all sounds and I don’t divide sounds into categories. Perhaps the elements you refer to is due to that it just fitted in the sound image.

gamemusic.pl: We’ve heard that on 3rd of December 2016 you are performing in Poland. Can you tell us a bit more about the event? Will that be your first time visiting our country?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): I was supposed to go there, but this event has been postponed for now and I have no new date, keep an eye on www.solarfields.com for updates regarding that.

gamemusic.pl: Your music to both Mirror’s Edge games redefined the electronic music genre, and not only in video games, but also in the electronic music world. What do you think made it so successful?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Perhaps it is due to that I make music that comes from me and my thoughts and I don’t care what’s going on in the music world and what’s popular. I do what I feel and stay true to myself.

gamemusic.pl: Mirror’s Edge Catalyst music is mostly focused on the pulsating bass, and only sometimes it’s a soft ambient. Could you shed some light on the process of creation of the soundtrack?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): When composed I had to keep in mind that all parts in the track had to work on its own since the audio engine in the game took care of the different layers like drums, percussion, ambiences, arpeggios. It is then up to the engine how the music is played back somehow.

„Not saying that all is boring today, but it is a little too much same out there.” – Solar Fields

gamemusic.pl: Did you take part in creation of the Warning Call song? How did you adapt it to the Mirror’s Edge Catalyst soundtrack?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): I am credited as a writer of the track, I can’t go into any more details, sorry about that.

gamemusic.pl: In your opinion, how much has the electronic music changed for the last 20 years? How do you take that kind of music now, as an artist?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): It has changed a lot in my ears, not to the good, today I feel that it is way less experimental in the electronic music compared to the ‘90s for example. Not saying that all is boring today, but it is a little too much same out there. People have the tools today to create whatever they really want to do and express them self deeply, but it rarely happens in my ears today.

gamemusic.pl: What would be your reaction to the statement that Mirror’s Edge Catalyst soundtrack is like a narcotic journey deep inside your own consciousness?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Skip the word narcotic and I’m with you. But yes, it is like a deep inner journey.

gamemusic.pl: In your opinion, what makes your music to both Mirror’s Edge games so well received by the fans?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Perhaps it is something fresh, when I compose music I don’t listen to what is popular or what’s going on in the music world, I just go with my inner feeling and transform it into sounds and music. My music is like a reflection of me, my mind, and my soul.

„Perhaps it is something fresh, when I compose music I don’t listen to what is popular or what’s going on in the music world, I just go with my inner feeling and transform it into sounds and music.”- Solar Fields

gamemusic.pl: How did you become so interested in synths and how has your passion influenced your life so far?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): In the late ‘70s when I was really young I started to play piano and was fascinated about the instrument, especially when played with the sustain pedal on, it gave me an atmosphere to the piano sound that I got hooked to. A couple of years later I got introduced to synthesizers and loved the possibilities. In the ‘80s I got my first own synthesizer and hooked it up to my Atari computer with the Steinberg Pro 24 software, and that’s how it started and I’ve been doing it since then.

gamemusic.pl: Mirror’s Edge music is very successful worldwide. Were you worried that you might not meet the expectations of fans before you started working on the music for Catalyst? If you were, how did you deal with the problem?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): I don’t think in that way when I compose, if I would do that I would have composed music for what I think the fans want to hear and then it wouldn’t be true to myself. I firstly create my vision of it, and if people love it, I’m deeply happy.

gamemusic.pl: Mirror’s Edge Catalyst music is over three-hour journey with your electronic music. Where do you get the ideas for particular sounds in your projects?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Soundtrack is actually +5 hours. It comes when I’m working on it, I’ve been working with synthesizers since the ‘80s as I wrote before, so I know how to re-create a sound I have in my head quite fast, and I also know what machine I need to use to get there. My brain is quite vivid so I hear my sounds clearly, and what I want to do.

„I firstly create my vision of it, and if people love it, I’m deeply happy.”- Solar Fields

gamemusic.pl: In your opinion, what is a determinant of a good soundtrack?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Something that reflects the spirit and the feeling of the game, so people can remember what happened in the game when they listen to a different parts of the music.

gamemusic.pl: What in your opinion was the most difficult stage during the production of the music for both instalments of Mirror’s Edge?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): I guess it is the deadlines, when I normally compose music I can take as much time I want for it, I can for example sit with just one sound for days and get it right. On project like this there is no time for all this long tweaking, so it was quite difficult in the beginning but after a while I got used to it and in the end all felt normal. Just needed to adjust my working method a little.

gamemusic.pl: Which moment of your career do you consider a break-through and why?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): When I decided to work full time on music and realize my dream and manage to live on it. To dare to take the plunge.

gamemusic.pl: Why, do you think, gamers like your music so much? What’s the secret?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Perhaps it is the peace of fresh air, something new for a lot of people and not the everyday music. I never look back on what I have done before, always moving on to new sound territories and explore that.

„On project like this there is no time for all this long tweaking, so it was quite difficult in the beginning but after a while I got used to it and in the end all felt normal.”- Solar Fields

gamemusic.pl: What in your opinion is so exceptional in Mirror’s Edge and in your music?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Perhaps the combination between the game and the music, they fit so well to each other and make it a new experience for the gamer.

gamemusic.pl: How do the soundtracks differ in terms of emotions and themes? Did you try to emphasize different aspects of Mirror’s Edge world? Or maybe the music came to being more ‘naturally’, without defined direction?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): When I was working on a mission for example, I did my study very carefully on each level by reading manuscript, meeting with level designers and the audio team at DICE. After that I knew what I basically needed to create to capture the feeling and mood for each level.

gamemusic.pl: Were you given a lot of freedom in composing music for Mirror’s Edge and the sequel?

Solar Fields (Magnus Birgersson): Yes, for sure, they wanted the Solar Fields sound on it so I got a lot of freedom. Of course everything has to work in the context so change was always in the process, but I could feel quite safe since it was my kind of sound they wanted.

Interview was conducted by Mariusz Borkowski.
